# What makes a Good Fallout project?

We’re giving you the opportunity and money to build hardware and electronics projects. Use it well\!

The best hardware projects are created when someone really wanted to make it. Maybe they saw it on YouTube, or it’s something from a really cool video game they play, or to solve a really niche problem.

\[img: comic\]

At the end of Fallout, we want you to be proud of the many projects you’ve built. We want you to be proud enough to share it with your friends, parents, teachers, and the world.

If someone searches your name and this pops up, you should be proud.

So… How do you get there?

<aside class="callout callout-info">

**Ask yourself: "Why am I building this?"**<br>If the **only** answer you can think of is "I want to go to Shenzhen," or if it's **only** "I want to learn how to use Arduino," there should be more to it.

</aside>

## A “Good” vs “Bad” Project

| Good ✅                                                                                                 | Bad ❌                                                                                              |
| :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| - Original idea with a story behind it<br>- Feels like a real product<br>- Specific and unique to you\! | - Copy-pasted design files<br>- Breadboarded and duct-taped<br>- Vague ("AI robot", "smart device") |

<aside class="callout callout-alert">

**In general, never copy paste stuff directly**<br>Use them as references and credit the authors if you do\!

</aside>

\[img: thought bubble\]

---

#### In summary:

**Be original.** Every idea has been thought of before. What matters is that it's _yours_. Give it a name, a personality, a brand.

**Be specific.** "AI robot" tells you nothing. "A robot trash can that follows floor lines and takes itself out when full" gives you a direction.

**Make it feel like a product.** No dangling wires. Add a case. Clean it up. It doesn't need to be perfect, but it should look intentional\!

### Here are some examples of what not to build

- Arduino alarm clock \- will you actually use this?
- AI healthcare robot \- what is this.
- Distance sensor \- does this serve a real purpose?

These are some ways you could make those ideas better:

- What if your alarm clock had a cool Silksong case? What if you had to play wack-a-mole to stop it? What if it had a speaker to tell you the weather every morning?
- What if the distance sensor alerts your phone when someone comes into your room?

\[img: little comic\]

Don’t overscope it\! Make it detailed. Your project should be mostly hardware, not software.
