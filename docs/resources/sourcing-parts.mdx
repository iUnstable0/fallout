## Specific parts

### General

Generally speaking, try to reference parts off of other projects since you'll eliminate more unknowns with the hardware. [Adafruit projects](https://learn.adafruit.com/) are a great source for this!

### Modules (TP4056, LED matrices, etc)

Almost always the cheapest option is to get it from [AliExpress](https://www.aliexpress.com/); the only downside is that shipping _may_ take awhile - usually a 2-3 week buffer is safe.

If you're in a pinch, Amazon actually has OK prices all things considered. I would avoid it though, unless you're going to miss the deadline to go IRL to Fallout if you don't use them

### SBCs (Raspberry Pi, Orange Pi, etc)

First off, do you actually need something this powerful? For a lot of applications (even ones that require display & camera processing) something like an ESP32 can do the trick, and it's much, much cheaper.

If you do need a Linux computer, a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W is the best price-to-performance SBC out there. If you need something more powerful, you can get Orange Pis from AliExpress.

Depending on your application though, you can also repurpose old laptops/desktops for your specific application if it's not space-sensitive.

### Microcontrollers (Arduino, RP2040, etc)

AliExpress is still usually your go-to for this! Getting a name brand product doesn't really matter, they're all going to work.

Arduinos are fairly outdated by todays standards, RP2040s, ESPs, are great alternatives.

### PCBs

In general, you should just choose the cheapest options that gives you a working PCB. Trade regulations keep changing, so the following advice might be outdated.

- If you're in India, use [SEEED Studio's Fusion Service](https://www.seeedstudio.com/fusion_pcb.html). They take HCB cards and are by far the cheapest option
- If you're from another country, use [JLCPCB](https://jlcpcb.com/), they offer PCBs for under $6 shipped as long as its under 100x100mm
- If you're from the US specifically, use [PCBWAY](https://www.pcbway.com/). They're roughly half the cost of JLCPCB because they're able to avoid tariffs somehow
  - Read here: [link](https://www.pcbway.com/blog/News/Impact_of_the_New_U_S_Tariff_Policy_on_Customs_Clearance_51dff4fd.html)
- EING is not allowed unless you have golden fingers (on-PCB USB-C contacts etc. and this is also subject to a case by case approval, please also mention it to your reviewer).
- Edge plating isn't allowed if it is only for the looks.

## Tips for specific vendors

### AliExpress

Aliexpress is one of the highest skill ceiling stores out there - there's a ton of optimisation you can do. Here's some tips:

- The welcome deal only works once when you make your account, so don't budget off of that - you will be hit by significantly higher prices afterwards.
- Pay attention to the estimate shipment date. This will vary by region, but generally speaking it's actually fairly reliable
- Also pay attention to the shipping cost! Usually items that don't have free shipping but a lower initial price actually end up being more expensive when you tally the costs
- There's specific vendors that are useful:
  - AITXEM ROBOT: Any microcontroller you can think of
  - Trianglelab: 3D printer parts!
