# How do I Get Money to Buy Parts?

After getting your project approved, you'll awarded with 7 Koi per hour of work. You can spend 7 Koi to get $5 USD in funding for your projects.

**This means that for every approved hour, you can get $5 USD towards your project.**

It does not mean that you should spend all of your money. The Koi you save can go towards travel stipends after you finish building your project IRL. Extra funds allow us to support future events like Fallout, and we encourage you to save where you can!

## What can I use the money for?

We're a nonprofit with limited resources, so please only use the money for the following:

- [x] Buying the minimum number of components & parts for your project
- [x] Buying the bare minimum of tools you need to build your project
- [x] Paying for the cheapest manufacturing option to get a working part (PCBs, etc.)

You MAY NOT use the money for:

- [ ] Buying extra components or parts that you don't need
- [ ] Buying extra parts "just in case" (e.g. a spare MCU, extra spool of filament)
- [ ] Buying extra tools that you don't need
- [ ] Buying fancy tools or parts that aren't necessary to build your project
- [ ] Paying for the expensive manufacturing option when a cheaper one would work
- [ ] Pre-built modules or kits that replace the parts you're supposed to design yourself
- [ ] Buying anything that isn't directly related to building your project
- [ ] Buying software licenses or subscriptions (e.g. Fusion 360, Adobe Creative Cloud, etc.)

<aside class="callout callout-danger">

**We monitor your transactions.** Misuse of your funds will lead to a ban from Fallout and potentially Hack Club as a whole. If you're not sure if something is an appropriate use of your funds, ask us before buying it!

</aside>

## How do I get the money?

We'll issue a grant card from our financial platform, [HCB](https://hcb.hackclub.com/), with the amount of money you have available. You can use this card to make purchases online or in-store (by adding it to a mobile wallet like Apple or Google Pay), just like a normal debit card.

<aside class="callout callout-alert">

When using the card, make sure to select "credit" at checkout, and to use the card details directly, instead of using a payment platform like PayPal or Razorpay, otherwise your transaction may be declined.

Please make sure to also use the correct billing address (both line 1 and line 2) when making purchases, otherwise your transaction may be declined.

</aside>

## Why do I need to upload receipts?

As a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, we're legally mandated by the IRS to have receipts for purchases. Please upload your receipts for the transactions as soon as possible. We also require you to upload receipts for all purchases made with your grant card to ensure that the money is being used appropriately. This also helps us keep track of how the money is being spent and allows us to provide support if you have any issues with your purchases.

<aside class="callout callout-alert">

Receipts need to show what is bought, quantity, price of each item, and the total. **A screenshot of the total is NOT enough.**

</aside>
