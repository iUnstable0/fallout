## The Five Steps to _Fallout_

The premise is simple:

1. Ideate
2. Design digitally
3. Get funding
4. Build IRL
5. Share with the world (aka online)

And.. Go to Shenzhen\!

I’ll explain in a minute how getting money to build / going to Shenzhen works.

### Step 1: The idea

Ever watched that one Youtube video and wondered – _"How the heck did they build that?"_

\[image youtube video I built a spaceship\]

Well, now’s your chance to start.

\[image: What do you want to make? LED keyboard / custom controller w/ silksong\]

It can be REALLY simple. In fact, if you’re new, it SHOULD be really simple. That’s how you improve.

\[image: graph\]

But it should feel closer to a product than a demo.

Your final goal is to build something others can understand, recreate, and improve it.

\[image: twitter post? understand, recreate, improve\]

Make it personal\! You’ll enjoy working on it and time will fly by. Don’t copy paste someone else’s work, or copy tutorials. It should be your project, not someone else’s, or AI’s.

### Step 2: Track your progress (Journal \+ Timelapse)

Two months ago, Sam and I called at 1AM. The conversation went something like this:

"Okay. We want to help people build sickass hardware projects. But how do we make sure they’re real?"

"Ummmm"

"We pray for the best?"

\[image: comic\]

We’ve been scammed before: thousands of dollars given to fake projects.

So… We settled on this: To get Koi (our currency), you’ll journal and timelapse your progress\!

#### TIMELAPSE

Screen record or film yourself through Lapse (Hack Club’s timelapse recording tool). It works in browser\!

If you’re feeling fancy, you can record or stream to YouTube with your own setup like OBS. Check out [How to Track Time](/docs/requirements/how-to-timelapse) for more details\!

#### JOURNAL

Journal your day’s work by clicking on a trunk after you:

- Reached a milestone
- Finished for the day

Be detailed, write about what you did, what you learned, or how you failed.

<aside class="callout callout-info">

**AI doesn’t know you.** It doesn’t matter if you’re “bad at writing,” just don’t use it. We have a [Good Journaling](/docs/requirements/good-journaling) page with some helpful tips\!

</aside>

\[image: Koi / timelapse / journal\]

You can exchange Koi Fish for hardware grants to build your project\!
